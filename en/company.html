Our heritage

The Chevalier Estate

Tenuta Chevalier is a unique and artisanal reality located in the Valpolicella Classica, in the municipalities of Marano and Negrar. We own 3 hectares of our own vineyards, all organic since 2009, and others that we are converting.

Through the knowledge of Dr. Paolo Zardini, agronomist and oenologist, owner of Tenuta Chevalier. A decisive step imposed by the property is that the company is moving towards a zero impact management system, sinking its vision into the study area of biodynamic and ecological cultivation. Use your head and knowledge is the business rule!

Our philosophy

Passion in Every Bottle

"The charm of a very high quality wine is the relationship that is created between what is found in the glass, and the person who relies on their senses to understand it; giving rise to an intimate and personal relationship."

Chevalier is a French name, by virtue of the fact that the first owner was indeed a French winemaker lady. The Estate which is located in the heart of Valpolicella and in one of the noblest areas of the Classica area. It can be said that Chevalier is located in one of the most pleasant areas where the traditions and culture of wine have their roots in the mists of time.

The producer

Dr. Paolo Zardini

Dr. Paolo Zardini, agronomist and oenologist, is the visionary behind Tenuta Chevalier. His expertise drives the estate's commitment to organic viticulture and the exploration of biodynamic principles.

Through rigorous study and a deep respect for the land, Dr. Zardini ensures that every bottle from Tenuta Chevalier reflects the unique terroir of Valpolicella Classica. His philosophy is simple yet profound: Use your head and knowledge is the business rule.

Our terroir

The Vineyards

Nestled in the rolling hills of Valpolicella Classica

Location

Municipalities of Negrar di Valpolicella and Marano di Valpolicella, in the heart of the historic Valpolicella Classica zone.

Altitude & Exposure

Approximately 350 meters above sea level, with optimal East-West exposure ensuring perfect sun ripening.

Soil

Various types of soil: clayey-silty, sometimes calcareous, volcanic or alluvial, giving unique character to our wines.

Cultivation

Organic since 2009. No chemicals are used — only Copper and Sulfur, following the 1885 formula by Pierre-Marie Alexis Millardet.

Training System

Guyot and double pergola, traditional vine training methods that have shaped Valpolicella wines for centuries.

Future

Moving towards zero-impact biodynamic and ecological cultivation, exploring innovative sustainable practices.
